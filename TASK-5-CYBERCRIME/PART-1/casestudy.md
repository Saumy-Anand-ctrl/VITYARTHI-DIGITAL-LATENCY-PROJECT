Case Study: Phishing Attack on a University Student

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords or financial details.


How it happens:

:-Attacker sends a fake email or message, appearing to be from a trusted source (e.g., university admin or bank).

:-Message creates urgency, asking the victim to "verify" account details or "reset" password.

:-Victim clicks the link, enters credentials on a fake website.Attacker captures login details, accesses accounts.


Targeted group:    Students, especially those using university emails, are common targets due to their limited experience with sophisticated scams.


Consequences:

:-Financial loss (if bank accounts are compromised)
:-Identity theft (if personal data is stolen)
:-Compromised academic records (if university accounts are hacked)
:-Emotional distress and loss of trust in online services

Example: A student receives an email "from" their university, asking to "verify" email credentials due to a "security update". 
The student enters details on the phishing site, and the attacker accesses their email and other linked accounts.

Phishing preys on trust and urgency . Always verify sender authenticity, avoid clicking suspicious links, and use two-factor authentication .
